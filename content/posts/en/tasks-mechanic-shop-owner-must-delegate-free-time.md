The routine of a mechanic shop owner almost always follows the same pattern: you arrive early, open the gate, help move cars, answer the phone, close quotes, buy parts, and still try to fix difficult cars. At the end of the day, the feeling is one of exhaustion, but with little money in the account and no free weekends.

For your shop to grow (and for you to have a personal life), you must understand that **you cannot be the best mechanic, the best salesperson, and the best manager all at once**.

Below, we list the 3 tasks you must delegate *immediately*.

## 1. Scheduling and Initial Triage

**Why you shouldn't do it:** Staying on WhatsApp scheduling a tune-up or oil change drains energy. You become the bottleneck. If you are busy buying parts, the client waits 3 hours for a reply.

**How to delegate:** You can hire a receptionist or, even better, use **CoreAutoCRM**. With our system's native Artificial Intelligence operating via WhatsApp, the client interacts directly, chooses an available date, and the system puts it on the calendar without you having to type a single "Good morning, what's the vehicle's license plate?".

## 2. Follow-up on Finished Cars

**Why you shouldn't do it:** Clients always want to know "Hey boss, can I pick up my Civic yet?". Replying one by one throughout the day completely breaks your train of thought on complex repairs.

**How to delegate:** Create a visual process (like a Kanban board). When the car changes phases (from *In Maintenance* to *Ready for Pickup*), the client should be notified—automatically.
In CoreAutoCRM, as soon as the responsible mechanic moves the vehicle's status on their phone, the client's WhatsApp automatically pings advising that the car is ready and detailing the final service cost. Zero effort from you.

## 3. Parts Purchasing and Quoting

**Why you shouldn't do it:** Spending hours on the phone quoting a timing belt price at 3 different auto parts stores is the job of a purchasing assistant, not the CEO of the shop.

**How to delegate:** Train a trusted person to handle only supplies. Establish clear rules: if the variation is up to X%, they can close without asking you. If it is over Y dollars, they ask for approval. Give the guidelines and step away from the petty penny negotiations.

## The Challenge: Trusting the Process

Many shop owners are afraid to delegate because they think "no one does it as well as I do." This is a process error, not a people error.

When you implement a robust system that standardizes the way your shop serves and works, it becomes easy to put any person—or Artificial Intelligence—in charge of operating it. By delegating these 3 areas, you will finally get your weekends and peace of mind back.
