If you walk through the shop floor of traditional auto repair shops, you will find the same scenario from decades ago: [clipboards hanging, the use of paper slips being a problem](/post/fim-papelzinho-ia-oficinas), scribbled whiteboards, and mechanics constantly asking "which is the next car?". This model not only wastes time but delays deliveries.

The modern solution for this is the **TV Dashboard in Cinema Mode**. Imagine a Smart TV fixed on the shop floor showing, in real time, exactly what everyone should do, without anyone needing to touch a keyboard.

## Why Abandon the Whiteboard?

Whiteboards require constant manual updating. If the advisor forgets to erase a car that has already been delivered, the entire shop floor loses sync. With an integrated system like CoreAutoCRM, the dashboard on the TV reflects the system's reality without delay.

Practical gains include:
- **Total transparency:** The mechanic looks up and knows what the priority is.
- **Friendly competition:** Everyone sees who is delivering more services.
- **Clean view:** Cinema Mode was designed with "Zero Scrollbars." Information pages automatically in a continuous loop of screens (carousel).

## 1. Team Heatmap

One of the most innovative features of the TV Dashboard is the **Workloads** screen. Each mechanic is treated logically as a physical ramp.

| Color on TV | Workload Meaning | Advisor Action |
| --------- | -------------------- | ----------------- |
| **Green** | Idle (0 active OS) | Assign new vehicle immediately |
| **Blue/Cyan** | Moderate (Up to 50% capacity) | Normal work flow |
| **Orange** | High (Up to 85%) | Caution before sending more cars |
| **Red (Pulsing)** | Critical (Over 85%) | Bottleneck! Help the mechanic or redirect the service |

This allows the shop manager to distribute services intelligently just by looking at the color of the mechanic's block on the TV.

## 2. Secure Long-Duration Authentication (Totem Mode)

A major problem with using TVs in the shop is that the system session expires and someone needs to grab a keyboard to log in again in the middle of the shift.

Advanced systems generate a **Long-Duration Token**. You configure the URL on the TV once, and it will remain authenticated and updating data uninterruptedly 24/7.

## 3. Dynamism That Captures Attention

A static screen ends up blending into the background; people stop looking at it.

The modern TV dashboard rotates every 12 seconds showing different screens:
1. **Workloads:** How the team is distributed.
2. **Mechanic Focus:** A giant screen highlighting the OSs of a single professional.
3. **Next Deliveries:** Cars that are in detailing or ready waiting for the customer.
4. **Overview:** The updated status of all ramps simultaneously.

## Start Using Visual Management

The implementation of a TV on the shop floor is, statistically, one of the fastest cultural changes in an auto shop. Visuals impact behavior instantly.

You don't need expensive infrastructure — an entry-level Smart TV and access to the CoreAutoCRM dashboard are enough to transform your common shop into a high-performance automotive center.
