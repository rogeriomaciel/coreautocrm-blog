The scenario is painfully common: a car owner contacts your shop on WhatsApp asking for a quote to replace their struts and shocks. You pause your work, calculate parts costs with suppliers, estimate labor, type out a detailed message, and hit send.

The customer reads it... and **simply never replies**. Radio silence.

Automotive repair industry studies show that up to **25% of a shop's potential revenue is lost** not because prices were too high, but because **no one followed up** on the sent estimate.

In this article, you will understand why prospective customers ghost your shop and how *automated follow-up* can recover those pending quotes without making you sound like a pushy salesperson.

## Why do customers ghost your shop on WhatsApp?

Most shop owners assume silence means a definitive "no" or that the customer went to a cheaper competitor. In reality, the most common reasons are:

1. **Distraction & Busy Routine:** The customer opened your message during work, thought the price was fair, but got pulled into a meeting and forgot to reply.
2. **Unanswered Technical Questions:** They didn't understand why the CV joint boots needed replacement alongside the axle and felt awkward asking.
3. **Unclear Payment Terms:** They liked the estimate but wanted to know if they could pay in installments.

If you don't send a gentle follow-up message, that customer is likely lost forever.

## The Perfect Automated Follow-up Structure

Effective follow-up isn't about calling every day to pester the customer. It's about offering assistance at the right moment. A successful sequence has 2 key steps:

### Lembrete 1 (24 hours after estimate sent)
A light, helpful check-in:
> *"Hi Robert, hope you're having a great day! I sent over the brake overhaul estimate yesterday. Did you have any questions about the parts list or payment options I can help with?"*

### Lembrete 2 (48 hours after estimate sent)
A friendly scheduling incentive:
> *"Hi Robert! I'm organizing our service bay schedule for the end of the week. If you'd like to proceed with the repair on your Focus, I have an open slot Thursday morning. We can hold current supplier pricing through today!"*
