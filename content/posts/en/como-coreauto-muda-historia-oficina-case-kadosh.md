CoreAutoCRM changes the history of an auto repair shop by **replacing the exhaustive data entry of traditional systems with a 100% conversational operation on WhatsApp**, recovering an average of 50% of estimates that were previously lost to oblivion, and organizing the shop floor visually and automatically. In the [Kadosh Automotive Maintenance Case Study](https://coreautocrm.com.br/case-kadosh/) (8 lifts and 7 mechanics in Aparecida de Goiânia - GO), the transition eliminated operational chaos and gave back the personal life of the partner, who now travels and goes fishing bi-weekly while the AI handles customer service, pricing, and triage.

Below, we detail how this transformation occurs in practice and which tools were co-created to structure this operation.

---

## Why do traditional ERPs generate chaos in the auto shop?

Common management systems require the team to spend the day typing data into complex screens. For the mechanic on the shop floor and the advisor at the counter, this manual process is slow and unfeasible. WhatsApp ends up turning into an disorganized repository of messages, while vehicles sit idle on lifts waiting for approvals. 

CoreAutoCRM solves this by inverting the role of WhatsApp: **it stops being a simple chat inbox and becomes the interface that commands the entire shop**.

---

## How does CoreAutoCRM's AI change the shop floor routine?

Artificial Intelligence acts as an invisible engine (Kadu Engine) that automates intake, diagnosis, and closing of services directly through the phone.

| Role | Traditional Operation (Chaos) | With CoreAutoCRM (Conversational Automation) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Customer** | Waits hours for a reply or receives a cold PDF estimate and disappears. | Instant service, automatic symptom triage, and active follow-up with AI. |
| **Mechanic** | Needs to drop tools to register parts and labor on the computer. | Sends a voice note on WhatsApp; the AI transcribes, interprets, and logs items to the OS automatically. |
| **Manager** | Constant nagging of the team and manual typing of received estimates. | Sends PDFs of other estimates for the AI to "magically" import in seconds. |

---

## What tools were created based on Kadosh's real case?

During deployment at the Kadosh shop, daily life on the shop floor with 10 to 20 work orders per day required the co-creation of features that are now part of the standard product:

* **Kanban by Mechanic (Labor):** Displays the total accumulated in labor (in R$) and the workload indicator for each mechanic in real-time, allowing bottlenecks to be managed and tasks to be distributed in a balanced way.
* **TV Dashboard in Cinema Mode:** An optimized screen (without vertical scrollbars) broadcasted on TVs on the shop floor, replacing the old whiteboard and allowing the whole team to visualize who is doing what.
* **Mechanic's Slip:** A transparent financial dashboard showing projected balance, unified commission, and individual ART (Average Repair Time) measurement.

---

## What were Kadosh's financial and human results?

The transformation at Kadosh was not limited to internal organization, but directly impacted the company's financial health and the partners' mental health:

1. **Recovery of 50% of Estimates:** The AI analyzes the lead queue and generates personalized follow-up suggestions in natural language (assisted follow-up), converting customers who had disappeared.
2. **Stabilized Revenue:** Revenue, previously limited by the bottleneck of human service, stabilized at new growth levels (with a targeted goal of R$ 500k/month).
3. **Management Freedom (The "Fishing" Indicator):** The success indicator most celebrated by the partners was the ability to be away from the shop every 15 days without the shop floor halting, tracking all activity from afar via mobile.
